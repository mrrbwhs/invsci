8.1 – How do science-related events affect society’s view of science?
  • 8.1.1 – investigate case studies of past events to consider how they have affected the public image of science, including but not limited to:
    • 8.1.1.1 – meltdowns of nuclear reactors
    • 8.1.1.2 – development of the smallpox vaccine
    • 8.1.1.3 – development of flight
    • 8.1.1.4 – positive and negative aspects of damming rivers
8.2 – Why is scientific research regulated?
  • 8.2.1 – investigate the need for the regulation of scientific research in, for example:
    • 8.2.1.1 – genetic modification of sex cells and embryos
    • 8.2.1.2 – development of biotechnological weaponry
    • 8.2.1.3 – testing of pharmaceuticals
    • 8.2.1.4 – products and processes of the nuclear industry
    • 8.2.1.5 – protection of Indigenous cultural and intellectual property
  • 8.2.2 – investigate and assess ethical issues surrounding current scientific research in, for example:
    • 8.2.2.1 – use of radiation
    • 8.2.2.2 – pharmaceutical research
    • 8.2.2.3 – gene manipulation in biotechnology
    • 8.2.2.4 – mining practices
    • 8.2.2.5 – bioprospecting
  • 8.2.3 – investigate a range of international scientific codes of conduct in regard to scientific research and practice in the areas of, for example:
    • 8.2.3.1 – cloning
    • 8.2.3.2 – stem cell research
    • 8.2.3.3 – surrogacy
    • 8.2.3.4 – genetically modified foods
    • 8.2.3.5 – transplantation of organs
  • 8.2.4 – evaluate the effectiveness of international regulation in scientific research and practice
8.3 – How do economic, social and political influences affect scientific research?
  • 8.3.1 – evaluate the costs involved in space exploration compared to investments in social issues, for example poverty and human global food supply
  • 8.3.2 – evaluate how scientific research aids economic development and human progress in relation to, for example:
    • 8.3.2.1 – nuclear power generation
    • 8.3.2.2 – use of antimicrobial drugs
    • 8.3.2.3 – genetically modified foods
    • 8.3.2.4 – use of petroleum products
    • 8.3.2.5 – robotics and the use of drones
  • 8.3.3 – evaluate the impacts of scientific research, devices and applications on world health and human wellbeing, including but not limited to:
    • 8.3.3.1 – medical surgical devices
    • 8.3.3.2 – surgical procedures
    • 8.3.3.3 – water purification and wastewater treatment
    • 8.3.3.4 – vaccination programs for the eradication of disease
  • 8.3.4 – using examples, analyse the impacts that governments and large corporations have on scientific research, including but not limited to:
    • 8.3.4.1 – corporations and market opportunities
    • 8.3.4.2 – university research project budgets
    • 8.3.4.3 – governmental budgets and limited time priorities
    • 8.3.4.4 – benefit-sharing in research using Indigenous intellectual and cultural property
  • 8.3.5 – evaluate how personal, cultural and socioeconomic perspectives can influence the direction of scientific research, for example:
    • 8.3.5.1 – perceptions about diet in a multicultural society
    • 8.3.5.2 – investigating traditional medical treatments
    • 8.3.5.3 – mining practices
